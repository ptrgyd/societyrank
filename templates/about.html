Elo scoring is the eponymous scoring system devised by chess master and physics professor Arpad Elo. If you have a score 400 points greater than your opponent, you are roughly 9 times more likely to win over your opponent.

So, for every one hundred games played between the 800-point player and 400-point player, ninety games (90%) will go to the 800 pointer.

Similarly, for every hundred games played between the 1200 pointer and 400 pointer, 99 will go to the 1200 pointer.

Thus, in SocietyRank, a difference of 400 means that for every ten hangs, any given individual is ~9x times more likely to select the higher scored society member. Fortunately, real-world hangs are not subject to such constraints. Happy ranking!
